CREED

Center for the Restoration of Economic Data

CREED is a Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia initiative I co-founded that transforms historical paper archives into research-ready datasets using OCR, digitization, and AI/ML techniques. The project combines large language models with established methods to extract structured data from historical documents at scale.

Our research demonstrates when modular LLM-based approaches outperform hyperspecialized models for historical document analysis, and the resulting datasets enable new research on topics including housing discrimination, wealth inequality, and economic history.

In August 2024, we hosted From Rasters to Rows: New Methods and Applications in Automated Data Extraction at the Philadelphia Fed, bringing together researchers working at the frontier of automated data extraction.
